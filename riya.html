What is Yoga?

yoga

Yoga is a kind of ancient sports or spiritual practices that connect mind and body together. Yoga originates in ancient Indian philosophy, and it has different types that combine different body positions, relaxation or meditation, as well as the use of different breathing methods. The determination depends on the degree of fitness of the body and the desired result of its practice. The most common type of yoga in Western culture is Hatha Yoga. Yoga can be said to be a physical exercise based on body positions to enhance the individual's control of his mind and body and to rest him. Yoga has six main branches: yoga, karma yoga, yoga, yoga, Hatha Yoga, and Tantra Yoga

Yoga is one of the most popular fitness exercises, especially in the United States. It is a means of enlightenment in Hindu, Jain, Buddhist and Sikh religions. It has had a great influence on the spiritual practices of other religions around the world.

Yoga is derived from Sanskrit yog, which means joining together. Some have translated it to mean the union of mind and body with each other. Yoga on the body can have many health benefits, such as: alleviating lower back pain, enhancing balance, increasing flexibility However, exercise should be exercised with caution. Repeated pressure on the neck, shoulders, legs, knees, and spine may cause bodily injury.
