the rules of football

Soccer is only the feet, and the goalkeeper is the only player allowed to touch the ball with his own hands within the limits of his team's box. FIFA is the only official reference to the rules governing the way football is played all over the world and the rules of the game:

  • Number of Players:

    Football matches are made up of two teams, each with 11 players on the pitch. Each team is allowed to switch 3 players during the game in official matches and an unlimited number of substitutions in friendly matches.

  • Goal:

    The goal consists of two vertical columns, 2.44 meters long, and a horizontal crossbar 7.32 meters long.

  • Football referees:

      Football matches are played by four referees: the referee of the main arena, the assistant referees, and a fourth referee outside the field; to help the three referees make decisions and compensate one of them in case of injury.

  • Cards:

    The system of cards is one of the most important disciplinary laws in football. The system is based on yellow and red cards, where the referee rules the yellow cards in cases of player riots, interference interfering with the game, causing harm to the players, wasting time, Which causes the players to be kicked out of the field in cases of very violent intervention, the player has two yellow cards, the beating of one of the players or the referees on the pitch, or the riot or racism that requires the referee to play the game ZAM.

Football Tournaments

There are many local, continental and international championships. The local championships are a long-term competition, with several local clubs competing with each other and crowning each other. The team that scored the most points at the end of the league season, each game wins the team adds to the score three points, and one point in the case of a draw, the loser does not get any point, and usually another tournament with the league championship called Cup, The contest competition of all state clubs of different local degrees among them; to obtain the title.

 The Champions League, the AFC Champions League, the African Champions League, and each country have a certain number of clubs allowed to participate in the tournament, depending on the country's classification issued by the International Federation of Football, The continental championships are characterized by great enthusiasm and passion, strong national motivation to represent the country to the world, and very difficult to achieve their titles. Along with these tournaments are two world championships: the World Cup and the Confederations Cup. These two championships are held every four years under certain conditions and controls.
