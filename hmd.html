Definition of civilization

Civilization has known many definitions by anthropologists. Ralph Beddington said that the civilization of any people is only a package of intellectual and material tools that enable this people to fulfill their social and vital needs satisfactorily and also enable it to adjust to its environment in a suitable manner. Edward Taylor also defined civilization as a composite of all the beliefs, values, traditions, laws, information, arts, and any habits, behaviors, or potentials that an individual can get in a society. In the end, civilization can be defined as the material and moral legacy of man in the past, which man has depended upon to complete his current life and progress, whether it is moral aspects such as life style, daily life, science, knowledge or material tools that have remained the same as bricks, , And various handicrafts; such as porcelain, pottery, and others.

The Rise of Civilizations

The emergence of civilizations on tribal bases, derived from the strength of the association of blood and kinship, so the tribe formed an urban area has many patterns, and habits of its own governing the behavior of its members in a single social format. It then began to evolve from tribal to religious, which in turn included many tribes. And then evolved into the political sphere that contributed to the integration of isolated groups with other groups.
